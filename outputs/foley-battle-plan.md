## THE APPROACH STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Warm Introduction (Week 1)

**Option A — Direct to Kurzon (preferred):**
Send a personalized email to Andrew Kurzon referencing Foley's recent fund closings, the Private Fund Managers Conference, and the specific idle capital math on a fund like Five Elms VI or Wincove II. Frame it as "I run a treasury infrastructure platform in Milwaukee and your clients are exactly the sponsors who benefit most."

**Option B — Milwaukee Entry Point:**
If direct outreach to Kurzon doesn't generate a response within 7 days, approach Andrew Wronski (Milwaukee Managing Partner) as a fellow Milwaukee business leader. Ask for an internal introduction to the FFIM practice co-chairs. The hometown angle is genuine and differentiating.

### Phase 2: Discovery Meeting (Week 2–3)

**Goal:** 30-minute call with Kurzon or Meli. Not a sales pitch — a strategic conversation.

**Agenda framing:**
1. "I've been studying what's happening at the intersection of fund formation and fund infrastructure" (2 min)
2. The Quiet Bleed — quantified for a Foley-typical client ($300M fund) (5 min)
3. How the platform works — the three-party structure, the Charles Schwab white-label, the compliance architecture (5 min)
4. What this means for Foley — client retention, value-add positioning, zero burden on the firm (5 min)
5. Open discussion (13 min)

**Key message:** "You're already the most trusted advisor your clients have during fund formation. We give you a reason to stay in the conversation after the fund closes."

### Phase 3: Conference Play (Ongoing)

**Target:** Foley's Third Annual Private Fund Managers Conference (likely Sept 2026).

**Ask:** A speaking slot or panel participation on "Fund Infrastructure Beyond the Close" or "Why Mid-Market Treasury is the Next LP Expectation." If a speaking slot isn't available, request attendance as a featured guest with the opportunity to connect with Foley's client network.

### Phase 4: Referral Pilot (Month 2–3)

**Propose a pilot:** Foley introduces HMG to 2–3 fund formation clients currently in market. HMG runs the Quiet Bleed analysis for each, and Foley sees firsthand how the introduction process works and what client feedback looks like.

**Success metric:** If even one of the three pilots converts, the economics prove the model and Foley has a repeatable value-add for every future fund formation engagement.

## OBJECTION PREP (FOLEY-SPECIFIC)

**"We don't make product referrals to clients."**
→ "This isn't a product referral. It's infrastructure guidance — no different from recommending a fund administrator or custodian during formation. Your clients are already making treasury decisions by default (leaving capital in sweep accounts). We help them make that decision intentionally."

**"Our clients already have treasury in place."**
→ "Most of them have a money market fund. That's a single instrument, not a treasury operation. No FDIC optimization, no branded LP portal, no reinvestment pathway. The gap between what they have and what Blackstone has is exactly what we fill."

**"What's in it for Foley?"**
→ "Client retention and value-add positioning. You become the advisor who brought them an idea worth $1–3M/year. That strengthens the relationship for the next fund, the next engagement, the next referral. There's no fee to Foley, no work required, and no compliance exposure."

**"How is this different from what Schwab/Fidelity already offers?"**
→ "Schwab is our custodian — they hold the assets. But Schwab doesn't provide the investment management, the FDIC optimization, the fund-specific reporting, or the white-label branding under the sponsor's name. We built the integration layer on top of Schwab's infrastructure."
